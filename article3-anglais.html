Desert flower

This book is an autobiography by Waris Dirie.
She tells us her story which begins in Somalia.
The autobiography begins at the age of 3 years.
In this article, we are going to turn to the first chapters.
The first chapters look at his childhood, around the age of 3 to 13 years old.

Going through her excision, rape, and forced marriage. His story inevitably reaches the sensitivity of readers, especially for people who have not experienced this kind of “tradition”.

Summary

The book begins when she meets a lion in the desert after she has run away from home, then we saw her in the past.
At the age of 3, she followed her mother, a friend and her sister to see what this famous passage of excision is to finally be a woman.
Having seen what it was, that did not stop this strong desire to be excised.

Her excision was at the age of 5, her mother held her against a rock with her legs and the old woman excised her.
She tells us that before excision, the woman takes out a damaged razor blade, with dried blood on it, and that she spat on the blade before starting.

Afterwards she was taken to a hut alone for 2 weeks, with her legs linked so that it would heal and not open.
She had a small infection but not too serious. However, one of her sisters and one of her cousins ​​died after excision.

After excision, the father protects his daughters from sexual relations and rape, so that they remain “intact” the day they marry another man.
They are preserved, the goal being that it is the husband who reopens the vagina on the wedding night.

At the age of 13, she wanted to marry a man who was interested in her sister, but one day, taking care of the camels her sister came to pick her up telling her that her father had a husband for her. Going to see the man, she saw that it was a 60 year old man. This man offered in exchange 5 camels.
She therefore decided to flee, in the middle of the night, she ran away from the hut, without any belongings, food or water.
The next day, her father catches up with her, but has not been able to bring her back
Another morning, when she woke up, she had a lion in front of her, but the lion did not eat her. Maybe out of pity or because there was nothing to eat on it.

Arriving on a road, she hitchhiked, one day a truck stopped.
Inside was the conductor and two men. She went alone to the back, over stones, but in the middle of the night, a man came towards her. He tried to rape her, but she hit him with a stone, and ran away.

After she finally managed to get to a town in Somalia, she looked for where her uncle lived.
A man offered to help him, took him home, gave him something to eat, and told him to go take a “nap”.
A few years before, a man had offered her the same thing and tried to rape her. So she flees.
On leaving, an old lady asked her who she was looking for, and accompanied her to her uncle.

To know the rest, I invite you to read the book Desert Flower, which is really interesting and teaches us a lot of things.
